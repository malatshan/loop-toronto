Why bins, not boxes

Cardboard is the weakest link in your move.

It collapses under weight. It soaks up rain. It runs out at 9pm the night before. And then it lives in your hallway until trash day. We just did the math and decided: nobody should have to do this.

01

No tape, no assembly

Snap-lock lids. Pre-cleaned. Walk in, pack, walk out — your evening doesn't disappear into folding flaps.

02

Waterproof and tough

Set them on a wet sidewalk, leave them in the lobby in March. Heavy-duty plastic, ~40 lb load each, no flex.

03

Identical and stackable

Every bin is the same size. They tessellate in elevators, dollies, and trucks. No more Tetris with random Amazon boxes.

04

Reused, not recycled

Each Loop bin runs 100+ moves before retirement. That's 100 fewer cardboard piles, 100 fewer Sunday recycling runs.

Real worries, real answers

The stuff people actually ask.

Filter by what you're worried about — kids, pets, walk-ups, weather, money, building rules. We've heard it.

BuildingI live in a walk-up, third floor. Is that a problem?
Honestly, the opposite — bins are a walk-up's best friend. They have proper handles, snap-lock lids that don't pop open mid-stair, and they don't bottom-out under a heavy load. We bring a hand truck if you're hauling them up yourself. Tell us in the booking notes, we'll plan accordingly.
BuildingMy condo requires a Certificate of Insurance and elevator booking. Can you handle that?
Yes. We have a standard COI we can email to your building manager the same morning, and we coordinate with concierge for service-elevator times. Most condo boards have seen our paperwork before — including the heritage buildings around College & Spadina, the Yorkville towers, and most of King West. Put your address in the booking and we'll handle the rest.
PeopleI'm moving with kids. What helps?
A few things. One: let kids decorate two or three bins as theirs — sticker, marker, whatever — so they have agency over the chaos. Two: we can leave a few bins early so the slow stuff (toys, books) can move at kid-pace. Three: the snap lids genuinely matter when you're at the new place at 9pm and a sleepy kid needs their stuffed animal — you'll find it.
PeopleI have a cat / dog / nervous animal. Anything to know?
Pets get rattled by movers and open doors. Two practical things: lids snap closed even with a curious paw on top (so a bin makes a fine temporary safe-space when filled with their bedding), and we always knock-and-wait rather than walking in. Tell us if you'd like us to leave the bins in the lobby instead of at your door.
WeatherIt's pouring rain on my move day. Now what?
Bins are a rainy-day upgrade. They don't soak through, lids stay sealed, and you can stack them dripping in your foyer without worrying about the floor. We bring extra grip pads in winter so they don't slide on icy lobby tile. The only thing rain can ruin is your mood — and the iced coffee waiting at the new place helps.
WeatherHow clean are the bins between rentals?
Wiped down with a vinegar-and-water solution. Lids and handles sanitized. Inspected for cracks and missing snap-locks. If a bin is anything less than ready-to-pack, it doesn't go to a customer. (We also invite you to text us a photo if anything seems off when they arrive — we'll swap it that day.)
LogisticsWhat if I need to keep the bins longer than expected?
Just message us. Extensions are $1.45/bin per additional week, charged at pickup. There's no penalty, no awkward reschedule fee. The first time we did rentals we forgot to charge anyone for extensions for two whole months — old habits.
LogisticsOne address for delivery, a different one for pickup — possible?
That's literally the whole point. Tell us both on the booking. We charge one delivery fee since it's one trip on our end. Most movers we serve are doing exactly this.
MoneyWhat happens if a bin gets damaged or lost?
Each rental includes a refundable $50 damage hold. If a bin is genuinely lost or unusable when we get it back, we deduct $40 per bin from the hold. Normal wear-and-tear is on us. We've never charged anyone the full $50 — and we've moved a lot of people.
MoneyIs this actually cheaper than cardboard?
For most 1BR moves, yes — by ten or twenty dollars. For studios, it's roughly even. The bigger savings: no time spent buying boxes, taping them, flattening them after, or driving them to a recycling depot. If you bill your time at anything more than minimum wage, it's not close.
LogisticsSame-day or next-day deliveries?
In the downtown core (M5/M4), next-day is usually fine and same-day is sometimes possible. Outside the core, we ask for 48 hours notice. The earlier you book, the better window we can offer.
WeatherDo I need to clean the bins before returning?
Just empty them. We sanitize everything ourselves. If a bin comes back with food spills or anything sticky, that's still fine — but please, no soggy sandwiches.
A note from the founder

I started Loop because moving broke me.

Four moves in five years — four different apartments, four bad weekends, four dumpsters worth of cardboard at the curb. By the third move I had this thought: I'm spending real money to make my life worse. Buy boxes. Build boxes. Fill boxes. Move boxes. Break down boxes. Drag them to the curb. Why is this still how we do it?

So we built Loop. The bins are the easy part. The hard part is being the kind of business you'd actually recommend to a friend at 10pm the night before their move — when they text you, panicked, that the boxes never came. We answer that text. We always have.

If anything about a Loop move is less than great, my number is on the booking confirmation. Text it. I'll fix it. That's the whole pitch.

— Sari
Founder · Loop
